Problem 1
$\displaystyle f(x) = \dfrac{x-2}{x^2-4}$
HoleVAHA: y=0
Factor: $\dfrac{(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)}$
Hole: $(x-2)$ cancels → Hole at $x=2$.  Reduced: $\dfrac{1}{x+2}$.  $f(2)=\dfrac{1}{2+2}=\dfrac{1}{4}$ → Hole at $\left(2,\,\dfrac{1}{4}\right)$
VA: $x+2=0$ → $x=-2$
HA: deg(num)=1 < deg(den)=2 → $y=0$
Direction near $x=-2$: $x\to-2^+$: $(x+2)\to0^+$, numerator$=1>0$ → $+\infty$;  $x\to-2^-$: → $-\infty$
Hole: $\left(2,\frac{1}{4}\right)$  |  VA: $x=-2$  |  HA: $y=0$
$f(x)=\frac{x-2}{x^2-4}$ — reduced: $\frac{1}{x+2}$, Hole $(2,\frac{1}{4})$, VA $x=-2$

Hole: $(-3,-6)$  |  No VA  |  Simplified: $y=x-3$

Problem 4  ★ Kenny's emphasis problem
$\displaystyle f(x) = \dfrac{x^2+x-6}{-x^2+3x}$
Two VAsNo HoleHA: y=−1
Factor: Numerator: $(x+3)(x-2)$
Denominator: $-x^2+3x = -x(x-3)$  (factor out $-x$)
So: $\dfrac{(x+3)(x-2)}{-x(x-3)}$
Hole check: No common factors between $(x+3)(x-2)$ and $-x(x-3)$ → No holes
VA: Denom $= 0$ when $x=0$ or $x=3$ → VA: $x=0$ and $x=3$
HA: deg(num)=2 $=$ deg(den)=2 → ratio of leading coefficients: $\dfrac{1}{-1} = -1$ → HA: $y=-1$
$x$-int: Numerator $=0$: $x=-3$ or $x=2$ → $(-3,\,0)$ and $(2,\,0)$
Note: The negative leading coefficient in the denominator flips the HA sign. Always factor out the negative carefully.
No hole  |  VA: $x=0,\;x=3$  |  HA: $y=-1$
$\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{-x(x-3)}$ — VAs $x=0,x=3$, HA $y=-1$
